Aloe Vera (Aloe barbadensis miller)

Succulent with fleshy, spiky leaves. Known for its medicinal properties, soothing sunburns and skin irritations. Requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil. Drought-tolerant plant, requiring minimal watering. Easy to propagate from offsets or pups. Adds a touch of greenery to sunny windowsills or indoor gardens. Symbolizes healing and resilience.

Bamboo Palm (Chamaedorea seifrizii)

Tropical palm with feathery fronds. Thrives in bright, indirect light but can tolerate lower light conditions. Requires consistently moist soil and high humidity. Filters indoor air pollutants, improving air quality. Adds a touch of tropical elegance to indoor spaces. Ideal for offices or living rooms with bright, indirect light. Symbolizes peace and tranquility.

Fiddle Leaf Fig (Ficus lyrata)

Popular houseplant with large, glossy leaves. Requires bright, indirect light to thrive. Sensitive to overwatering, preferring slightly dry conditions. Adds a statement to any room with its elegant foliage. Prone to leaf drop if environmental conditions change abruptly. Benefits from occasional misting to increase humidity. Symbolizes beauty and elegance.

Calathea (Calathea spp.)

Striking foliage with intricate patterns. Requires bright, indirect light and high humidity to thrive. Sensitive to overwatering and prefers consistently moist soil. Adds a tropical flair to indoor spaces with its vibrant leaves. Benefits from regular misting to increase humidity levels. Tolerant of lower light conditions but may lose its markings. Symbolizes beauty and diversity in nature.

Chinese Money Plant (Pilea peperomioides)

Features round, coin-shaped leaves on slender stems. Thrives in bright, indirect light but tolerates lower light levels. Requires well-draining soil and moderate watering. Easy to propagate from offsets or pups. Adds a playful touch to any room with its unique foliage. Symbolizes prosperity and good fortune. Popular among plant enthusiasts for its distinctive appearance.

Monstera (Monstera deliciosa)

Iconic plant with large, fenestrated leaves. Thrives in bright, indirect light but tolerates lower light levels. Requires well-draining soil and occasional pruning to control growth. Adds a tropical vibe to indoor spaces. Easy to propagate from stem cuttings. Known for its striking foliage, adding visual interest to any room. Symbolizes growth and abundance.
